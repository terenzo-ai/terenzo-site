AI audit

What is an AI operations audit?

An AI operations audit is a two-week diagnostic. We map where AI and better systems will move the numbers in your business, rank the opportunities by impact and effort, and hand you a phased roadmap with year-one ROI. It is how Terenzo starts every engagement: diagnose first, prescribe second, maintain third.

What you get

A short deck that pays for itself.

01

Opportunity matrix

Every opportunity we find, plotted by business impact against effort to build. Quick wins first, big swings named, dead ends ruled out.

02

Phased roadmap

Quick wins in the first 30 to 90 days, larger builds in months four to eight, with an owner and a sequence you can actually run.

03

Year-one ROI

The money slide. Hours saved, dollars freed, and the payback math for each recommendation, in numbers you can take to the board.

How it works

Two weeks, start to roadmap.

  1. Discovery interviews

    Week one. We interview leadership for the strategy and budget, and the team for the on-the-ground reality. The gap between the two is where the value hides.

  2. Opportunity mapping

    Week two. We turn the interviews into an opportunity canvas, scoring each workflow on time saved and rework avoided, then plot impact against effort.

  3. Validation

    We walk the findings through your stakeholders before we present them. By the end, the roadmap is one your team helped build, not one handed down.

  4. The readout

    A short executive deck: the opportunity matrix, the phased roadmap, and the year-one ROI. Every recommendation backed by a number.

What it costs

Fixed fee, priced to the size of the company.

A small-company audit typically runs $5,000 to $10,000. A mid-market audit runs $25,000 to $50,000, with 10 to 15 interviews and deeper process mapping. Implementation is delivered through vetted partners and quoted after the audit. A maintenance retainer keeps the work current.

  3. Do you implement, or just advise?
